Please read these instructions carefully. There will be comprehension checks. If you fail these, you will be excluded from the study and only receive the fixed reward of {{Constants.base_pay}} dollar.

In this study, you will see various lotteries, all of which pay different amounts of money with different probabilities.

For the lottery decisions, we use a fictitious monetary unit called coins. After the experiment, the coins will be converted into US-dollar. Here {{exchange_rate}} coins are equivalent to one US-dollar.

An example lottery is:

Lottery
Probability 40 % 30 % 30 %
Payoff 20 coins 40 coins 60 coins

This means that the lottery pays either 20 coins or 40 coins or 60 coins (with different probabilities), but a lottery always only pays out one of the amounts. The lotteries will actually be played out by the computer and determine your earnings in exactly the way we describe below.

In total, we have created {{Constants.n_lotteries}} lotteries. For each lottery, we will ask you two questions. First, we will ask you how often you think you would win the highest payoff of the lottery. After that, we will ask you for the maximum price you are willing to pay for each lottery.

Please indicate your true maximum price for each lottery. A payment mechanism will determine your exact payoff following the experiment. Note that the payment mechanism used gives you an incentive to indicate your true maximum price. If you enter a lower maximum price, you will rarely receive the lottery. If you enter a higher maximum price, you will receive the lottery too often. If you are interested, you can view the exact details of how the payment mechanism works at the bottom of the page.

At the end of the experiment, one of the lotteries you rated will be randomly selected for the additional payoff. One out every thirty subjects will be randomly selected to receive an additional bonus.
